Ordo Privacy Policy

Effective July 9, 2026

Ordo is a daily social ranking game. This policy explains what we collect and how it's used. Short version: the minimum needed to run the game, nothing sold, no ads, no tracking.

What we collect

  • Account: an anonymous account ID created when you first open the app. Optionally, if you use Sign in with Apple, your Apple identifier (we never see your password; email is optional and may be Apple's private relay).
  • Your content: your username, rankings, guesses, votes, takes (comments), groups you create or join, and superlative ballots.

What we don't collect

No location, no contacts, no browsing history, no advertising identifiers, no analytics trackers, no selling of data — to anyone, ever.

How your content is shared

Rankings, takes, and game results are shared only within groups you choose to join. Daily aggregates are computed within your group. Nothing is public.

Where it lives

Data is stored with Supabase (hosted Postgres, US region) over encrypted connections.

Moderation & safety

Text goes through a profanity filter. You can block or report members in any group; reports come to us for review.

Deleting your data

Profile → Delete my account wipes your account, rankings, takes, and group memberships immediately. No email required, no waiting period.

Children

Ordo is not directed at children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect their data.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, we'll note it here with a new effective date.
